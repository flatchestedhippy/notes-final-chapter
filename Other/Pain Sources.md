### **Your Own Life**
- Things that feel harder than they should
- Repeated annoyances or workarounds you’ve built
- Tasks you mentally dread starting
- Confusion, delays, or unclear instructions
- Anything that made you think “this is stupid”
- Things you’ve Googled more than once

### **Your Son / Parenting**
- Moments where your son gets frustrated or confused
- Systems designed for adults but used by kids
- School portals, homework tools, communication gaps
- Scheduling, forms, permissions, logistics
- Places where you have to step in repeatedly
    
### **Work / Professional Life**
- Repeated clarifications in meetings or email
- Tasks that require rework
- Poor onboarding (tools, people, processes)
- Friction between systems (hand-offs, integrations)
- Tools people complain about but “just live with”
- Processes that depend on tribal knowledge
    
### **Bureaucracy & Institutions**
- Court systems
- School districts
- Healthcare portals
- Insurance processes
- Banking, billing, payments
- Government forms and notices
- Identity verification and account recovery

### **Household / Physical World**
- Home repairs and maintenance
- DIY instructions that assume too much knowledge
- Tools or parts that don’t fit as expected
- Installations that are unclear or error-prone
- Yard work, HOA rules, utilities
- Appliance setup or troubleshooting

### **Other People (Quiet Observation)**
- What people complain about casually
- Where people seem stressed or confused
- Repeated questions someone asks
- Frustration masked as jokes or sarcasm
- Situations where someone says “sorry to bother you, but…”

### **Products & Services (Without Social Media)**
- Software you personally use
- Subscriptions you tolerate rather than love
- Customer support experiences
- Billing or cancellation processes
- Instructions, documentation, help centers
    
### **Heuristics to Trigger Pain Recognition**
- “Why is this so hard?”
- “This should be easier.”
- “There has to be a better way.”
- “Why do I have to do this manually?”
- “Why didn’t they think of this?”
    

If you hear or think any of these → **log the pain**.
